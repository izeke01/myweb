Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  • 1. What is IJARERS?
    IJARERS stands for the International Journal of Advanced Research in Life, Environmental, Physical & Engineering Sciences. It is a multidisciplinary, peer-reviewed, open access journal.
  • 2. What subjects does IJARERS cover?
    IJARERS covers Life Sciences, Environmental Sciences, Physical Sciences, Engineering Sciences, and interdisciplinary areas such as Bioinformatics, Biophysics, and Data Science.
  • 3. How often is the journal published?
    IJARERS is published monthly.
  • 4. Is IJARERS an open access journal?
    Yes, all articles published in IJARERS are open access and freely available online.
  • 5. How can I submit my manuscript?
    Manuscripts can be submitted by email to editor@ijarers.com. Please follow the submission guidelines provided on the website.
  • 6. What file format should my submission be in?
    Submissions must be in Microsoft Word format (*.doc or *.docx).
  • 7. Is there a template for manuscript submission?
    Yes, authors should use the IJARERS template available for download on the Submission Guidelines page.
  • 8. What is the peer review process?
    All submissions undergo double-blind peer review by at least two independent reviewers based on originality and quality.
  • 9. How long does the review process take?
    The review process typically takes 4–8 weeks, depending on reviewer availability and response time.
  • 10. Are there any publication charges or fees?
    Please refer to the journal website or contact the editorial office for information about publication fees.
  • 11. What is the similarity/plagiarism policy?
    Manuscripts must have a similarity index of 20% or less, checked using iThenticate or Turnitin. Reports must be submitted with the manuscript.
  • 12. What citation style should I use?
    IJARERS requires IEEE citation style. Use reference management tools like Mendeley, EndNote, or Zotero.
  • 13. Can I suggest reviewers for my manuscript?
    Yes, authors are encouraged to suggest up to three qualified reviewers with their names, affiliations, and official email addresses.
  • 14. What is the policy on conflicts of interest?
    Authors, editors, and reviewers must disclose any potential conflicts of interest. The editorial board will resolve any issues that arise.
  • 15. What types of articles does IJARERS accept?
    IJARERS accepts original research articles, review articles, and short communications in relevant fields.
  • 16. How do I prepare figures and tables?
    Figures and tables should be high-resolution, numbered, and include descriptive titles. Preferred formats are TIFF, JPEG, PNG, GIF, or vector-based files (minimum 300 dpi).
  • 17. What is the copyright policy?
    Upon acceptance, authors grant IJARERS exclusive publication rights. All articles are published under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International License (CC BY-SA 4.0).
  • 18. How can I track the status of my submission?
    Authors will be notified by email regarding the status of their submission at each stage of the review and publication process.
  • 19. Is there a word limit for manuscripts?
    There is no strict word limit, but manuscripts should be concise and follow the formatting guidelines provided by IJARERS.
  • 20. How do I contact the editorial office?
    You can contact the editorial office by email at editor@ijarers.com or through the Contact page on the journal website.
  • 21. What is the language of publication?
    All papers must be written and published in English.
  • 22. Is IJARERS indexed?
    IJARERS is indexed in Crossref, Publons, Google Scholar, CiteSeer, Academia.edu, ICI Journals Master, and DRJI.
  • 23. Can I reuse my published article?
    Yes, authors may reuse their published articles with proper acknowledgement, in accordance with the CC BY-SA 4.0 license.
  • 24. What is the process for submitting a revised manuscript?
    Authors must highlight all changes, provide a response letter to reviewers, and submit both the revised manuscript and response letter as separate files.
  • 25. Who is on the editorial committee?
    The editorial committee consists of experienced academics from leading Indian and Pakistani universities. See the Editorial Committee page for details.
